YEARS of Mr LEROLE

A celebration of Elias Boy Shamber Lerole’s 70 years of making music,
passion for music and an instrument called the Pennywhistle,  

Elias started playing the Pennywhistle in 1947 and in 1949 he moved to Moria Boyne, ZCC church to attend school and this is where he was exposed to playing many wind-instruments such as the Trombone, Tuba,
Trumpet, Saxophone etc. 

With all the education and exposure of playing different instruments Elias excelled. Years later he moved back to Joburg and never hesitated on composing many songs. Elias recorded Kwela Biza as his first recorded song, which became a hit but didn't match his all time world hit "TOM HARK". 
 
Tom Hark by Elias & His Zig Zag Jive Flutes became a world renowned hit and a classic; and it is still the most re-recorded Kwela song by many artists abroad, but unfortunately in Apartheid years musicians were
robbed and didn't receive credit and royalties for their compositions.

Elias is the founder of Alexander Shamber Boys, Black Mambazo, Nkintsho Brothers, Shamba Jazzman, Elias & His Zig Zag Jive Flutes, Dark City Sisters, Walter and The Beggers, Abafana Base Goli, IsiDlodlo, Kwela Tebza etc.

The Lerole family will celebrate and honor Elias Shamber Boy Lerole at his 80th
birthday this year, "70 years of Legendary Pennywhistling" is a story we will
share with South Africans and the world about his legendary journey. 

Elias Shamber Boy Lerole and many Pennywhistlers and Kwela musicians such
as Spokes Mashiyane, Lemmy Mabaso, Big Voice Jack Lerole, Peter “Black
Duke” Makane,  David Ramosa, Zeph Nkabinde, Simon “Mahlathini’
Nkabinde, Aaron Shongwe, Willard Cele, Abia Themba, Cox Masinga to
